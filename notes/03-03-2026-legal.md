# Contact Law

## Contact

A legally enforcable agreement between two or more parties

## Contract Law

Specifies what constitutes a legally enforceable agreement

## Breach of Contract

When one party fails to follow the terms of a contract

## Contract Requirements

1. An offer is made
2. Voluntary acceptance of offer
3. Both parties give consideration
4. Both parties are competent
   - Legal age
   - Sound mind and body
5. The contract covers a legal act
6. The contract is in the proper form

## Revokable trust

- Assets are placed in trust
- Allows to avoid probate court - when family member dies without will, court decides how to distribute inheritance
  - Debts must be paid first before inheritance can he distributed
- Trust has will
  - Assets
  - Heirs
- Bank account - can have payable upon death
- Car - can have tranfer upon death

## Irrevokable trust

- Cannot be changed

> **Aposiled copy:** government verified as official document

## Breached Contracts

If a contract is breached, the following may be ordered

- Specific performance
- Payment of damages
- Discharge of obligation

# Antitrust Legislation

**The Federal Trade Commission Act of 1914** created the FTC and prohibits unfair methods of competition in commerce

**The Wheeler Lea Amendment of 1938** gave the FTC additional congrol over false and misleading ads

**The Robinson-Patman Act of 1936** prohibits price discrimination and applies to both buyers and sellers

## History of High Profile Antitrust Cases

- United States v. Standard Oil 1911
- United States v. American Tobacco 1911
- United States v. E.I. du Pont de Nemours 1961
- United States v. AT&T 1982
- United States v. Microsoft 2000
