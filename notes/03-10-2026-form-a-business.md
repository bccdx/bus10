## Sole Proprietership

Business owned and usually managed by one person / family

- 72% of businesses
- 15% of total receipts

### Benefits

1. easy to start / end
2. being your own boss
3. pride of ownership
4. leaving a legacy
5. retention of company profit0
6. no special taxes

### Disadvantages

1. unlimited liability
2. limited financial resources
3. management difficulties
4. overwhelming time commitment
5. few fringe benefits
6. limited growth
7. limited lifespan
8. unk unk - unknown unknowns

### Process

1. go to city of Berkeley, get a business permit
2. get North American Industrial Classification System (NAICS) - 6 digits

- mixed residential
  - single family + apartments
- mixed use
  - shops, restaurant, housing
- industrial
- parks

## Partnership

2+ people legally agree to become co-owners of a business

- eg: licensed professionals such as doctors
- 8% of businesses
- 6% of total receipts

### General Partnership

All owners share in operating the business and in assuming liability for the business's debts

### General Partner

owner who has unlimited liability and is active in managing the firm

### Limited Partnership

A partnership with one or more general partners and one or more limited partners

### Limited Partner

### Master Limited Partnership

A partnership that looks much like a corp, but is taxes like a partnership and thus avoids the corporate income tax

### Limited Liability Partnership

Limits partners' risk of losing their personal assets to the outcomes of only their own acts and omissions and those of people under their supervision

### Benefits of Partnerships

- more financial resources
- shared managements and pooled skills and knowledge
- longer survival
- no special taxes

### Corporation Taxes

```
revenue - cost of goods sold = Gross profit
gross profit - operating expenses = net income
net income before taxes - 25% tax = net income after tax
```

### Net Income After Tax

1. reinvest in business (retained earnings)
2. dividends - paid to stock holders
3. stock buyback

## Corporation

A legal entity with authority to act and have liability apart from its owners

- Corporate veil - "wall" to insulate owners from liabilities of business
  - Owners / Stockholders | Business
- 20% of businesses
- 81% of total receipts
