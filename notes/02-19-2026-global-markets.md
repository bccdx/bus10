# Global Markets

## Importing

Buying products from another country

## Exporting

Selling products to another country

> The US is the largest importing and second largest exporting nation in the world

Countries with abundant natural resources like Venezuela or Russia need technological resources from other counrties like Japan

Global trade allows countries to produce what they make best and buy what they need from others

## Free Trade

The movement of goods and services among nations without political or economic barriers

## Pros and Cons of Free Trade

| Pros                                                               | Cons                                                |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| global market has 7 bil+ customers                                 | domestic workers can lose jobs to outsourcing       |
| productivity ^ countries produce at comparitive advantage          | workers may get pay cuts                            |
| global competition + cheap imports = prices low, counter inflation | loss of service jobs, increase in white collar jobs |
| encourages competition,innovation, economic growth                 | domestic companies lose comparitive advantage       |
| access to foreign investments, interest rates low                  |                                                     |

## Comparitive Advantage

A country exports the products it produces efficiently and imports the products it does not produce efficiently

## Absolute Advantage

A country has a monopoly on producing a specific product or is able to produce it more efficiently than all other countries

### Small Business + Global Trade

% of US small businesses export, but account for 30% of exports

## Measure Global Trade

### Balance of Trade

Total value of exports v. imports over a particular period

### Trade Surplus (Favorable)

\$ Exports > $ Imports

### Trade Deficit (Unfavorable)

\$ Exports < $ Imports

### Balance of Payments

Difference between money in (from exports) v. money out (from imports), goal is to have more money in than out (favorable balance)

### Unfair Trade Practices
#### Dumping
Selling products in a foreign country at lower prices than those charged in the producing country, dumping is prohibited

China, Brazil and Russia have been penalized for dumping steel in the United States.
