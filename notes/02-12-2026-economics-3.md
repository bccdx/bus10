# Gross Domestic Product

GDP is the total value of final goods and services produced in a country in a given year. As long as a company is within a country's border, their numbers contribute to the country's GDP, even if they are foreign-owned

When GDP changes, businesses feel the effect

Data comes from Bureau of Economic Analysis: BEA.gov

## Consumer Price Index

Monthly stats that measure the pace of inflation or deflation

The government computres the cost of goods and services to see if they are going up or down

The wages, rents. tax brackets, government benefits, and interest rates of some citizens are based on the CPI

## Producer Price Index

The same is done for producers / companies

Measures cost of components to produce products

# Unemployment Rate

Percent of people who are 16 years+, umemployed, and actively looking for a job in the past 4 weeks

### Types of Unemployment

- Frictional
  - "In between jobs" - possibly have another job lined up but not started yet
  - Generally not a sign of an unhealthy economy
- Structural
  - Mismatch between jobs that are available and skills people have
  - More of a problem
- Cyclical
  - Up and down that coincides with the economy
  - Lower when economy is expanding, higher when economy is contracting / in a recession (2 quarters of negative GDP)
- Seasonal
  - Unemployment that coincides with seasons, such as agricultural work, summer / winter jobs, etc.

### Underemployment Rate

Situation where one has technical skills but works in a lower-skilled field due to difficulty finding a job in the desired field

> Not counted in the unemployment rate

Statistics are tracked but generally not published

# Inflation

The rise in prices of goods and services over time

## Disinflation

When the price increases are slowing (inflation rate declining)

## Deflation

Prices are declining because too few dollars are chasing too many goods

## Stagflation

Economy is slowing, but prices are going up

## Federal Reserve

- Controls interest rates
  - Lowering interest rate will stimulate the economy and increase inflation
  - Raising the interst rate will slow down the economy and reduce inflation
- Controls supply of money
  - Can set reserve requirements
  - Forces banks to reserve percent of money and is not allowed to lend it out
  - If inflation is too high, reserve requirement can be set higher

### Tariffs

Tax on imports, paid by citizens of country that implements tariff, with the goal of increasing domestic production
